Biography

Jingjing Zhao is an Associate Professor with Beihang University, Beijing. She received the B.S. degree from Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Beijing, China, in 2013, and the Ph.D. degree from the Queen Mary University of London, London, U.K., in 2017. From 2017 to 2018, she was a Post-Doctoral Research Fellow with the Department of Informatics, King’s College London, London. From 2018 to 2020, she was an AI Researcher with Amazon, London.

Dr. Zhao serves as an "Outstanding Hundred Talents" Associate Professor and PhD supervisor at Beihang University. She is the Principal Investigator for projects funded by the National Natural Science Foundation of China, the National Key R&D Program of China, and the Civil Aviation Key Laboratory Foundation. Dr. Zhao previously served as the Chinese representative for the ITU's air-to-ground communication topic and is currently an expert on the ICAO Communication Panel. She also serves as an Editor for IEEE Transactions on Communications.

To Prospective Students for 26 Fall

I'm always looking for self-motivated students who want to pursue a Ph.D degree on 6G research directions, including but not limited to the following:

  • Pinching-Antenna Systems (PASS)
  • Movable-Element Simultaneously Transmitting and Reflecting Surface (ME-STARS)
  • Air-to-Ground Integrated Sensing and Communications (ISAC)
  • AI-based Air-to-Ground Communications

Students with strong backgrounds in wireless communications, signal processing, mathematical optimization, and machine learning are encouraged to apply. If you are interested in joining us, please send your CV, transcript, and ranking to me via email.

I also welcome applications for post-doctoral research or visiting students/scholars.
